The five limbs

Understanding what each element of the Panchang means for daily life.

1st Limb
Vara
The day of the week, each ruled by a planet. Sunday by the Sun, Monday by the Moon, and so on — governing the general energy of the day.
2nd Limb
Tithi
The lunar day, defined as every 12° the Moon travels ahead of the Sun. 30 tithis complete one lunar month — 15 Shukla (waxing), 15 Krishna (waning).
3rd Limb
Nakshatra
The Moon's position among 27 lunar mansions, each spanning 13°20'. Each nakshatra has a ruling deity, planet, and quality that flavours the day's energy.
4th Limb
Yoga
Computed from the sum of the Sun and Moon's longitudes. 27 yogas, each with a specific character — from Vishkumbha (inauspicious) to Siddhi (perfection).
5th Limb
Karana
Half a Tithi — every 6° advance of the Moon. 11 karanas repeat in a defined cycle, with 4 fixed and 7 movable, governing the energy of half-day periods.
+ Muhurta
Rahu Kala
The 1/8th of the daytime ruled by Rahu — traditionally inauspicious for starting new ventures. Varies by weekday and geographic location.
